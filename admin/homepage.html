How The Calculation Works

Our system uses a sophisticated process to provide you with an accurate and optimized route plan. Here's a breakdown of the data and methods we use:

  • The Optimization Algorithm
    We employ a Genetic Algorithm (GA) to solve this complex routing challenge, which is a version of the classic Traveling Salesperson Problem (TSP). Inspired by natural selection, the GA intelligently searches through a vast number of possible route combinations to find the most efficient sequence of ports, saving you from having to test them all manually.
  • Distance Calculation
    To ensure accuracy, our system doesn't just measure straight lines. It uses the Haversine formula, which calculates the great-circle distance (the shortest path on a sphere) between ports using their precise latitude and longitude coordinates.
  • Travel Time Calculation
    Travel time is calculated by dividing the total distance by an average vessel speed. This speed is derived from the specific fuel consumption curve data you provided for the selected vessel, ensuring the estimate is tailored to that ship's performance characteristics.
  • Fuel Consumption Calculation
    Fuel usage is determined by multiplying the total distance by a fuel rate (e.g., tons per kilometer). This rate is also extracted from the vessel's unique fuel consumption curve, which directly links the ship's speed to its rate of fuel burn.
  • CO₂ Emissions Calculation
    This is a direct calculation: the total fuel consumed is multiplied by the specific CO₂ factor associated with the vessel's designated fuel type (e.g., Heavy Fuel Oil, Marine Gas Oil). This provides an accurate estimate of the voyage's carbon footprint.

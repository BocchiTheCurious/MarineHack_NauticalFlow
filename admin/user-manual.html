Optimized Route Planning

Learn how to create optimal maritime routes that minimize fuel consumption and CO₂ emissions while considering port congestion, weather conditions, and vessel capabilities.

How to Plan Optimal Routes
Duration: 8:15
Route Planning Steps
1
Access Route Planner
Click on "Optimized Route Planner" from the main navigation menu to open the planning interface.
2
Select Departure & Destination Ports
Choose your starting port and final destination from the dropdown menus. Add intermediate stops if needed.
3
Choose Your Vessel
Select the cruise ship from your fleet. The system will use its fuel consumption curve for calculations.
4
Set Departure Date & Time
Specify when you plan to depart. This affects weather and port congestion predictions.
5
Run Optimization
Click "Optimize Route" button. The algorithm will calculate the most efficient path considering all factors.
6
Review & Save Results
Analyze fuel savings, CO₂ reduction, and time estimates. Save the optimization for future reference.
Best Practice

Always check port weather conditions before finalizing your route. Severe weather can impact fuel efficiency significantly.

Managing Your Data

Effectively manage your vessel fleet, fuel types, and port information. Keep your data up-to-date to ensure accurate route optimization calculations and better decision-making.

Data Management Tutorial
Duration: 6:45
Data Management Guide
1
Adding Vessel Data
Navigate to Vessel Data page, click "Add New Vessel", fill in ship details, gross tonnage, and fuel consumption curve.

Click the edit icon on any record to modify details. Changes are saved immediately and affect future optimizations.

Deleting Records
Use the delete button with caution. Confirm deletion when prompted. Deleted data cannot be recovered.

Deleting a vessel or fuel type that's used in saved optimizations may affect historical data. Always backup important information before deletion.

Port Weather Monitoring

Monitor real-time weather conditions and safety assessments for any port in your network. The Port Weather feature provides comprehensive meteorological data, wave conditions, and automated safety evaluations to help you make informed operational decisions.

Using Port Weather
1
Access Port Weather
Navigate to "Port Weather" from the Live Data section in the sidebar menu.
2
Select Your Port
Choose a port from the dropdown menu to view its current weather conditions and forecasts.
3
Review Safety Assessment
Check the overall safety status banner (Safe, Caution, or Dangerous) and review any active warnings.
4
Analyze Current Conditions
View real-time data including temperature, wind speed, wave height, wave period, and wave direction with safety indicators.
5
Check 48-Hour Forecast
Review the hourly forecast cards showing conditions at +6, +12, +24, and +48 hours with color-coded safety borders.
6
Understand Safety Thresholds
Review the Safety Threshold Guidelines section to understand the criteria used for safety assessments.
Safety Features

The Port Weather system includes automated safety assessments based on:

  • Wave Height: Safe (<2m), Caution (2-4m), Dangerous (>4m)
  • Wind Speed: Safe (<40 km/h), Caution (40-60 km/h), Dangerous (>60 km/h)
  • Temperature: Safe (10-35°C), Caution (outside this range)
Pro Tip

Weather data is automatically refreshed from Open-Meteo and Marine Weather API. Click the "Refresh" button to get the latest updates, especially before planning departures or arrivals.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about NauticalFlow. If you can't find what you're looking for, please contact our support team.

How accurate are the fuel consumption calculations?

Our calculations are based on the fuel consumption curves you provide for each vessel, combined with real-time weather data and port congestion information. Accuracy typically ranges from 92-98% when vessel data is properly calibrated.

Can I optimize routes with multiple destination ports?

Yes! Our multi-port optimization feature uses advanced algorithms (Genetic Algorithm for TSP) to find the most efficient sequence of ports to visit, minimizing total fuel consumption and travel time.

What weather data sources does NauticalFlow use?

We integrate with Open-Meteo and Marine Weather API to provide real-time weather forecasts, wave conditions, wind patterns, and cyclone tracking for accurate route planning.

How do I update my vessel's fuel consumption curve?

Navigate to the Vessel Data page, find your vessel, and click the edit button. You can update the fuel consumption curve data points, which will be used in all future route optimizations.

Can I export my optimization history?

Yes! You can export your data in multiple formats including PDF reports for presentations and CSV files for further analysis in spreadsheet applications.

Is my data secure?

Yes, we take data security seriously. All data is encrypted in transit and at rest. We use industry-standard authentication protocols and regularly backup all information. Your vessel and route data remains private and confidential.

What happens if I delete a vessel that's used in saved optimizations?

Historical optimization records will retain the vessel name, but you won't be able to use that vessel for new optimizations. We recommend archiving vessels instead of deleting them if they have historical data.

How often is port and weather data updated?

Port data is updated manually as needed. Weather data is refreshed every 6 hours from our API sources, ensuring you always have current information for route planning decisions.
